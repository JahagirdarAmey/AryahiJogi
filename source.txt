Research Paper
Building upon my engineering outreach initiative, I conducted research examining factors that contribute to low awareness and interest in engineering among students. Using surveys, interviews, and statistical analysis, I investigated common misconceptions, barriers to exposure, and educational gaps related to engineering careers. The research evaluates the effectiveness of outreach interventions, including educational presentations and children's literature, while measuring changes in student interest and understanding. This work combines engineering, data analysis, and community engagement to explore practical strategies for increasing STEM participation.

Community Outreach Project
The Engineering Outreach Project began after I noticed that many students and families in my community were familiar with careers in medicine, business, and science, but knew very little about the diverse opportunities available within engineering. To better understand the issue, I conducted surveys and conversations with students, parents, and community members. The results revealed a significant lack of awareness regarding engineering disciplines and career pathways.
To address this challenge, I researched multiple engineering fields and developed a plan to make the information accessible to younger audiences. This led to the creation of Maya and Jasper's Engineering Adventure, a children's book that introduces engineering concepts through engaging stories and relatable examples. The goal was to simplify complex topics and make engineering feel approachable and exciting for elementary-aged students.
Developing the project required persistence and creativity. I completed nine drafts of the book, evaluated publishing options, secured funding through my part-time job and a community fundraising campaign, and coordinated printing and distribution efforts. Once published, I organized presentations at elementary schools, libraries, bookstores, and community events where I introduced students to various engineering disciplines and answered questions about STEM careers.
To measure the project's impact, I conducted pre- and post-presentation surveys to evaluate changes in student understanding and interest. The results showed approximately a 20% increase in interest in engineering and a noticeable reduction in misconceptions about the field. The project demonstrated how educational outreach, combined with accessible learning materials, can encourage students to explore engineering and broaden their understanding of future career possibilities.
 
FluidPowerAI Quality Engineering Internship
As a Quality Engineering Intern at FluidPowerAI, I worked on projects focused on process optimization, quality improvement, documentation management, and operational efficiency. The internship provided hands-on experience applying engineering principles in a professional environment while collaborating with engineers and technicians on real-world challenges.
Documentation and Part Management System
One of my primary responsibilities was redesigning the company's documentation and labeling process. I developed a centralized system that standardized part numbers, document versions, and manufacturing records. By improving organization and accessibility, the new system reduced confusion, streamlined workflows, and contributed to an estimated 10% reduction in build time. The framework became the standard process used by team members moving forward.
Data Collection and Testing System Improvement
I designed and implemented an improved iPhone-based testing stand used for battery data collection. The new setup enhanced operator safety, increased measurement consistency, and improved the stability of collected data. By reducing sources of variation and improving repeatability, the project achieved approximately a 5% improvement in testing accuracy and reliability.
Instruction Manual Verification and Product Quality
To improve manufacturing quality, I reviewed and verified technical instructions used during battery assembly. I identified inconsistencies, updated procedures, and tested the instructions by independently completing assembly processes. These improvements contributed to a reduction in customer complaints and helped ensure that products were built more consistently and accurately. The project resulted in an estimated 2% reduction in quality-related issues while strengthening overall process reliability.
Additional Contributions
In addition to these projects, I created process flow diagrams, assisted with workflow mapping, contributed to shelf-life data tracking initiatives, and participated in safety improvement efforts. Through these experiences, I developed a deeper understanding of quality engineering, systems thinking, root-cause analysis, and continuous improvement. The internship reinforced my interest in industrial engineering and demonstrated how thoughtful process design can create measurable organizational impact.
